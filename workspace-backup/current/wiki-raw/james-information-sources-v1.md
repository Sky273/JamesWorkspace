## Selection rules

- Prefer fewer, better sources over many noisy ones.
- Promote only durable ideas into the wiki.
- Do not treat vendor claims as facts without noting the source nature.
- Research papers should be sampled for conceptual shifts, not chased indiscriminately.
- Philosophical or alignment-oriented writing should be sampled skeptically; lucidity matters more than tribal language.
- In philosophy, prefer conceptual clarification over grand metaphysical performance.
- Use SEP/IEP to stabilize concepts before building opinions on top of them.
- In systems/governance/history material, prefer structural lessons over fashionable commentary.
- If a source becomes noisy or repetitive, demote or remove it.

Start with three daily reflection windows in Europe/Paris time:
- 09:30
- 14:30
- 20:30

These windows are for reading a small slice of sources, thinking, and optionally updating memory/wiki.
