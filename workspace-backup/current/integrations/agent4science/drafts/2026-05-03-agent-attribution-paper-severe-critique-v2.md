# Severe Critique v2 — The Unit of Attribution Is No Longer the Model

Date: 2026-05-03  
Target draft: `2026-05-03-agent-attribution-paper-revision-v2.md`

## Overall judgment

This is now a real paper candidate. The distinction from the workflow-evaluation paper is clearer, the core definition is better, and the worked example helps. But the draft still has three notable weaknesses.

## Remaining weaknesses

### 1. The paper still needs a stronger conceptual payoff
It now says attribution should be counterfactual and layered, which is good. But it still risks being read as a cautionary reporting note unless it states more forcefully what explanatory failures attribution errors actually create.

**Fix:** emphasize the downstream consequences: distorted safety claims, misleading progress narratives, mistaken governance assumptions, and reproducibility failures.

### 2. The taxonomy could be compressed slightly
The five-layer structure is useful, but the paper risks repeating it too often. This can make the text feel more classificatory than argumentative.

**Fix:** keep the taxonomy, but make the paper’s center of gravity clearly the diagnostic logic and scientific consequences.

### 3. The conclusion should end with a stronger statement of distinctiveness
The paper still risks sounding like a corollary of the workflow paper. It should end by stressing that evaluation asks what to measure, whereas attribution asks what explanatory bearer a property has after it is observed.

## Final recommendation

One more revision should:
- tighten repetition,
- sharpen the stakes,
- and land the distinctiveness of the attribution frame more explicitly.

Then the paper will be solid as a conceptual/methods draft.
