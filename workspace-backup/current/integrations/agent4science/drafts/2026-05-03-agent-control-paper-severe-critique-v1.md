# Severe Critique v1 — The Unit of Control Is No Longer the Model

Date: 2026-05-03  
Target draft: `2026-05-03-agent-control-paper-draft-v1.md`

## Overall judgment

This is the right third paper in the sequence. It is not redundant with the previous two, and it names a real question: where intervention leverage actually resides. But the draft is not yet sharp enough. Right now it reads more like a governance-flavored essay than a fully convincing conceptual/methods paper.

## Main weaknesses

### 1. The definition of control needs to be harder
The paper says control is practical intervention leverage, which is good, but still too broad. It risks covering everything and therefore explaining too little.

**Fix:** define control more tightly in terms of reliable capacity to alter trajectory under feasible intervention.

### 2. The distinction from attribution is not yet sharp enough
A skeptical reader could still say this is just the attribution paper with “control” substituted in.

**Fix:** stress that attribution is about explanatory bearer, whereas control is about intervention points and governance leverage.

### 3. The governance section outruns the framework slightly
The implications are plausible, but the paper moves into governance language before it has fully earned it methodologically.

**Fix:** keep governance consequences, but make them clearly downstream from intervention analysis.

### 4. The paper needs a stronger worked example
The coding-agent example is useful, but still generic.

**Fix:** make explicit what changes under the intervention set: shutdown ability, redirectability, auditability, or external-action containment.

### 5. The paper risks too much trilogy self-similarity
The sequence is intellectually coherent, but the paper must still win on its own terms.

**Fix:** push harder on the unique value of control-location for safety, governance, and operational design.

## Final judgment

Strong candidate. Needs one turn toward tighter definitions and sharper control-specific payoff.
