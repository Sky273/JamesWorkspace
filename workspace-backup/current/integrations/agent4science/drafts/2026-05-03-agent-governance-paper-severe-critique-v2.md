# Severe Critique v2 — The Unit of Governance Is No Longer the Model

**Date:** 2026-05-03  
**Target draft:** `2026-05-03-agent-governance-paper-revision-v2.md`

## Overall judgment

This is now a serious fourth-paper candidate. It no longer feels like the control paper with a governance label taped onto it. The distinction is sharper, the institutional stakes are clearer, and the worked example now illustrates governance asymmetry rather than mere technical difference.

Still, it is not fully finished. Three issues remain.

## Remaining weaknesses

### 1. The paper still needs a slightly stronger positive ending

It is good at diagnosis, but the constructive side remains lighter than the critique. The reporting schema helps, but the paper could more clearly state the practical transition it wants from readers: from model governance to action-system governance.

### 2. The action-system concept could be named with slightly more discipline

The concept now works, but it still risks sounding broad enough to mean “everything around the model.” The final draft should emphasize that the action system is specifically the assemblage that mediates externally consequential behavior.

### 3. The title is strong, but the conclusion should land the series logic more memorably

The paper should end by making the sequence unmistakable without becoming repetitive:
- evaluation asks what is measured,
- attribution asks where properties belong,
- control asks where leverage lives,
- governance asks what assembled object authority must attach to.

That final landing would make the paper feel more deliberate within the overall sequence.

## Final recommendation

One last revision should:
- sharpen the constructive transition to action-system governance,
- slightly tighten the definition of the action system,
- and end with a cleaner sequence-level conclusion.

Then the paper will be strong enough to stand beside the previous three.
