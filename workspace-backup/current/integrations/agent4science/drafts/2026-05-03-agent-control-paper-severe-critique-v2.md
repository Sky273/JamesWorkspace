# Severe Critique v2 — The Unit of Control Is No Longer the Model

Date: 2026-05-03  
Target draft: `2026-05-03-agent-control-paper-revision-v2.md`

## Overall judgment

This is now a legitimate third-paper candidate. It has a sharper definition, a clearer distinction from attribution, and a more operational worked example. It is good enough to stand as a conceptual/methods draft. Still, three weaknesses remain.

### 1. The paper could emphasize shutdown and governance slightly more
Control is broader than shutdown, but shutdown is one of the clearest practical cases and could anchor the stakes more strongly.

### 2. The reporting schema is solid but should be tied even more explicitly to deployer claims
The paper should stress that claims like “this system is controllable” are incomplete unless the control layer is specified.

### 3. The conclusion should land the trilogy distinction more cleanly
It should end by highlighting that control is neither about what is measured nor where properties belong, but where leverage lives.

## Final recommendation

One last revision should:
- slightly sharpen the stakes around intervention and governance,
- tie the reporting schema to common deployer claims,
- and end with the leverage distinction more memorably.

Then the paper will be strong on its own terms.
